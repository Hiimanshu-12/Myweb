What is Cricket?

Cricket is a bat-and-ball game that involves two teams of eleven players each. It is played on a large oval field with a pitch at the center. The game has a rich history, especially in countries like India, England, Australia, and South Africa.

History of Cricket

Cricket was first played in England in the 16th century, and it became a popular sport during the 18th century. The International Cricket Council (ICC) was formed in 1909, and cricket became an official part of the Olympic Games in 1900. Today, it is one of the most popular sports in the world.

Interesting Cricket Facts

  • The longest match in cricket history was played in 1939 between England and South Africa, lasting for 10 days.
  • The fastest century in ODIs is held by AB de Villiers, who scored it in just 31 balls.
  • Cricket is played in over 120 countries, and it has a global following of over 2.5 billion people.
